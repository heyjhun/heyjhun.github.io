When I was younger I always dream of working in an office. With desk, computer in front of me, working on papers, having meetings. I always thought that when I work as an office boy, it is something that would make me happy. I will earn money, I will be a boss. 

Right after graduation, I transferred from my province to the city.  I found my first job, it is a BPO company. It was something close to what I thought of being a great office boy. I would take phone calls, answer to their questions, update the system. Have lunchbreak with officemates, have a conversation, back to work. Repeat. 

After few weeks, oops, false alarm, it was not what I am looking for. I don't see myself working for this for a little longer. It is not me. I gave my resignation letter. 

After a few weeks, I applied again. Still not giving up with my dreams, the best office boy you could employed.

I applied to security systems provider company. Oh it is techy, I think I'm gonna like it. CCTV, Door access, computer systems. It is perfect for me. I did not graduate Information Technology for nothing. On my first day, I was introduced to the rest of the employees. I was sitting on my table. I was waiting for my very first task. After few minutes, my supervisor arrived. Configure this. I was given a door access, make it work he said. I was clueless at that time. I didnt kwnow what to do. I opened the box, I saw a parts of the door access system, and a manual to make it work. The manual is so detailed, I made it worked. I installed it on our office door. I informed my boss, he said "Good job". I was so happy, I finished my very first task. The day ends. Tomorrow is another day, my second day. I was given another task, a CCTV. It was kind of the same, configure, install. Just make it work. The next day, it was a different electronics again. I am enjoying this, everyday there is something different that I enjoy to do. Something new to learn. 

Then, few months later, I realized, I've been doing the same thing over and over again. I am not learning new things again. It was CCTV, door system, GPS, biometrics and the list goes on., but it was all the same. I was getting bored, I was getting exhausted. Looking at this technologies over and over again. I wanna learn new things again. After seven months, I left the company. I am looking for something new again.

I took a rest. I went back to the province. I just want to be with my family for few weeks. Oops, I enjoyed it. I took a rest for almost 3 months. Reality strike, I used up all of my savings. I have to go back. Find a job, earn money again. I wanna try again. Let's see this time.

I sent a resume to this Japanese Company. A chance to go to Japan. Wow, my eyes has been stuck to the job ads. I hit Apply. 

After 1 day I got an email from the HR. Please come for an interview, and I did. The Japanese boss interviewed me together with the HR whom I though was just an intern. She looked like a student.

The interview went well. Actually, I was not confident I nailed it. The boss has so many questions making me feel like do I deserve to be hired. After a week, I got an email, you passed. You will undergo training. The day has passed since the my first day. Everything went well. I did not even realize I was turning 5 years. I was so happy with the friends I made. I was happy with my achievements. It was a slow one, but this is based on my plan.  And by the way, I got that chance to be sent to Japan. Sugoidesu! Thankful for this company.

Then again, I grew tired of the work. If it weren't for the people I was working with, I would have resigned earlier than my 5 years. This was the longest. But just so you know, this has been my happiest stay in a company. 


You see the cycle there? Find a job, if you're not happy anymore, you resign, you find another job. And the cycle goes on and on. Why is it not easy to find the right one for you? Is there a certain type of people that really does not fit well in a company setting? Maybe I am just thinking well enuogh. Maybe my mind is wired this way. Finding a working environment that fits me well is time consuming. After 8 years being in the work-force, I am not even close to my dream. Now I am thinking, corporate world is not for everyone. At least maybe for me, I think I am not for a corporate setting. Maybe I need to be my own boss. Just maybe. See you soon when I find the best thing that suits me well. Until then, I will end this writing right here. Ciao.
