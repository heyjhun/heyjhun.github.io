Why are we afraid of trying out something new?

Simply put, why are we afraid of the things not known by us?

I heard from a Youtube video about [Rapid Transformational Therapy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iCmjA_sM4Nk){:target="_blank" rel="nofollow" title="Free Yourself From The Fear Of Judgement & Start Living Life | Marisa Peer
"} by Marissa Peer that when we were still babies or toddlers, we were not afraid of almost all the things that could pique our curiosity.


Then, as we grow older, we started to learn to be afraid of something. Slowly we were realizing that there were things that make us not want to try. When we were babies, we were not afraid to stick out fingers in the mouth of a dog because we do not know dogs can bite. We were not afraid of running here and there without knowing that we can fall or hit the ground and get hurt. Thus, it makes sense when I heard from Marisa Peer that fear is an acquired state.

![image-title-here](https://cdn.pixabay.com/photo/2016/11/14/03/38/achieve-1822503_960_720.jpg){:class="img-responsive" alt="girl-leaps-on-a-hill"}

We fear something because we have a preconceived notion of a possible outcome, and we are afraid of it. Afraid of getting the negative result and not the good side of it. And if we already know that there is a chance that we can end up failing, we would be afraid to even try.

Lately, I have been thinking to try this opportunity, something different to me, something I have been curious about trying out. This would help me get out of my comfort zone, but I fear not being able to get through once I started. There is a lot more to think of why I am afraid, something that someone may have experienced yet I see them sailing well.

One of my realization after watching this video, I want to be that kid again, unafraid to try regardless of what could be the outcome. I want to take on this without thinking about the fear of failure. If I fail, I will learn something new. If I succeed, I would still learn. And it will go on and on and on.

Sabi nga ni Will Smith:
> "The best things in life is placed on the other side of terror. On the other side of your maximum fear are all of the best things in life."
