Why are we afraid of trying out something new?

Simply put, why are we afraid of the things not known by us?

I hae read somewhere that when we were still babies or toddlers, we were not afraid of almost all the things that could peaked our curiousity.

Then, as we grow older, we started to learn to be afraid of something. Slowly we realized that there are things that makes us not want to try. When we were babies, we were not afraid to stick out fingers in the mounth of a dog because we do not know dogs can bite. We were not afraid of running here an there withouot knowing that we can fall or hit the ground and get hurt. Thus, it makes sense to say that fear is an acquired state.

We fear of something becuase we have a pre conceive notion of a possible outcome, and we are afraid of it. Afraid of getting the negative reult and not the good side of it. 

Lately, I have been thinking to try this opportunity, something different to me, something I have been curious at trying out. This would help get out of my comfort zone, but I fear of not being able to get through once I started. There is a lot more to think of why I am afraid, something that someone may have experienced yet I see them sailing good.

Now, I want to be that kid again, unafraid to try regardless of what could be the outcome. I want to take on this without thinking the fear of failure. If I fail, I will learn something new. If I succeed, I would still learn. And it will goes on and on and on. 

Sabi nga ni Will Smith:
"The best things in life is placed on the other side of terror. On the other side of your maximum fear are all of the best things in life."
